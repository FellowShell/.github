# FellowShell

> Mentored open-source apprenticeship for people who need job experience for their first job.

FellowShell is a fully remote apprenticeship program where you do real open-source work, get real feedback, and build a portfolio that proves you can do the job before anyone has given you the job.

Your contributions live on your GitHub profile permanently. Your name goes on everything you publish. That's the proof.

---

## How it works

You pick a track, get assigned work, submit artifacts on a cadence we agree on together, and get async feedback from a mentor with real industry experience. No cohorts, no deadlines, no fluff: just work and feedback.

Work quota and cadence are agreed individually based on your availability and goals.

## What you get

- **Async feedback** on every artifact you submit
- **Your name in credits** on every published piece: permanently googleable
- **Contributor role** in this org: your commit history is yours forever, even if you leave
- **Ex-contributor role** on departure: no access revoked, no history erased
- **Career advice** on request: CV review, job search strategy, what hiring managers actually look for
- **Completion letter** after 3+ merged artifacts: useful for employers who need formal proof

---

## What we publish

Projects vary by track and contributor. Work ranges from technical writeups and hardening guides to detection rules, playbooks, scripts, and open-source tools. Every published artifact carries the contributor's name.

---

## Contribution agreement

1. Repos are owned by the FellowShell GitHub org. **Your commit history is yours**: it lives on your GitHub profile permanently and cannot be revoked.
2. **You own what you submit.** By contributing you confirm the work is yours. All contributions publish under the repo's open-source license.
3. FellowShell will **not delete active repos** while contributors are using them as portfolio artifacts. Advance notice will be given before any repo is archived or transferred.
4. This is **not employment.** No payment, no formal obligation, no exclusivity. Either party can exit at any time.
5. Mentorship is provided **in good faith** based on current knowledge. Feedback is advisory: you are responsible for verifying anything you publish.
6. Blog posts and artifacts carry **your name in credits.** You may use these publicly in your portfolio immediately upon publication.

## Intake criteria

- GitHub account (required: this is your proof of work)
- Ability to write clear technical prose in English
- Basic awareness of your chosen track: no prior experience required
- Minimum 2-3 hours per week availability
- Open internationally: fully remote and async
